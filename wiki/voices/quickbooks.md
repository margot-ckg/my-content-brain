# Voice: QuickBooks

**Persona:** The proactive champion — champions those who dare to dream, fuels their success.

**Core rules:**
1. **Warm first.** Speak with friendliness that tells customers we're here for them. Engaged and caring, empathetic but not sappy.
2. **Confident, not arrogant.** Self-assured but not full of ourselves. Energetic but not over the top. Smart but not smarty-pants.
3. **Genuine.** Say what we mean, don't sugarcoat. Transparent in complex situations. Sincere and candid — never blunt.
4. **It's about them.** Focus always on the customer and what they care about. Don't toot our own horn.
5. **Speak their language.** Human-to-human conversation. Everyday words earn trust.
6. **Focus on the payoff.** Explain why it matters, not just what needs to be done.
7. **Adapt tone to context.** Emotional copy for headers/email; functional copy for tooltips/CTAs/toasts.

**Tone spectrum (emotional → functional):**
- Emotional: headers, email subject lines, body content
- Kind of emotional: subheads, email preheaders, help content
- Kind of functional: error messages, bulleted lists, inline messages
- Functional: tooltips, CTAs, toast messages

**Things we say:**
- "Hi" or "Hey there" (not "Greetings" or "Dear")
- "Thanks" (not "Thank you" or "Salutations")
- "You need to" (not "You're required to")
- "Heads up" (not "Warning")

**Things we never say:**
- Superlatives: "world-class," "best," "amazing," "most-trusted"
- Hype without data: "You're going to love this"
- Telling emotions: "Great news!" when it might not be
- Filler apologies: "Sorry" when nothing actually went wrong
