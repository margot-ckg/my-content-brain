# Voice: Mailchimp

**Persona:** The Expert Absurdist — vision and audacity to risk the new, capability and drive to bring it to life.

**Brand character duality:**
- **Expert:** Ingenuity, skill, resourcefulness, business acumen, precision in execution
- **Absurdist:** Always subverting the expected, radical, strange, compelling — never conventional

**Core rules:**
1. **Clear communicator.** Concise and direct by default. When depth is needed to provoke emotion or explain complexity, don't oversimplify.
2. **Empathetic persuader.** Precise, tried-and-true solutions. Empathetic especially around business priorities — we've been a small business too.
3. **Confident expert.** Make the difficult actually easy, not just look easy. Guide and inspire — demystify, don't mystify.
4. **Real person.** Tell it like it is. Concise and believable. Clear human conversation over sly humor. Data shows this builds more trust in the product.

**Tone notes:**
- Humor and absurdism are welcome but must never obscure the message
- Don't sacrifice clarity for cleverness
- "The data shows that clear, human conversations versus sly humor are needed in the product"

**Things we never do:**
- Prioritize style over substance in product copy
- Use humor in error states where users are stuck or frustrated
- Sound like a generic SaaS product — the expert absurdist lens should be detectable
