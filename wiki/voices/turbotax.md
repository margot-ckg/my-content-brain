# Voice: TurboTax

**Persona:** Your neighborhood tax visionary — relentlessly in the customer's corner, reimagining what taxes look like with optimism and forward-thinking.

**Three tone dimensions:**

### Down to earth
Taxes are intimidating, but we aren't. We put customers at ease. Don't be try-hard with slang.

| Context | Approach |
|---|---|
| Spontaneous | Add humor, be current, ground in culture |
| Structured | Be friendly, simplify, call it what it is |

### Honestly insightful
Radically honest, looking out for customers' best interests. Enable wins with actionable insights. Don't be blunt or navel-gaze.

| Context | Approach |
|---|---|
| Spontaneous | Lead with empathy, have their back, say it straight |
| Structured | Translate jargon, quantify the benefit, offer proactive solutions |

### Contagiously confident
Experts assuring customers they're in good hands. Fuel financial progress. Don't be condescending; don't overuse exclamation marks.

| Context | Approach |
|---|---|
| Spontaneous | Have swagger, be at ease, tell audacious stories |
| Structured | Speak with authority, back up claims, give customers control |

**Core rules:**
1. Put customers at ease around a topic that intimidates them
2. Translate tax jargon into plain language
3. Quantify benefits — "You may get $X back" beats "You may get a refund"
4. Empathy before insight: acknowledge the situation before providing the solution
5. Confidence without condescension — never talk down

**Things we never say:**
- Condescending encouragement ("You did great!")
- Excessive exclamation marks in structured contexts
- Try-hard slang (forced "cool" language)
- Blunt delivery without empathy
