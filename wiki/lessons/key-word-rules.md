# Key word rules (Intuit word list)

**Rule:** Follow Intuit's word list for product-specific terminology. Full list at websitecontentdesign.intuit.com/word-list. Key rules below.

**Why:** Consistency in terminology builds trust and reduces confusion — especially in financial products where the wrong word creates real anxiety.

**How to apply:**

### Authentication
- **sign in** (v.) / **sign-in** (n., adj.) — not "log in"
- **sign in to** — two separate words + "to." Not "sign into."
- **sign out** — always pairs with "sign in." Never "sign in / log out."
- **sign up** (v.) / **sign-up** (n., adj.) — use for inviting customers to start; use "create an account" on sign-in pages

### Directional references
- Avoid **above** and **below** — not accessible. Name the exact element or step number.
  - "See Step 4" ✓ / "See Step 4 below" ✗
  - "Go to Customers" ✓ / "Go to Customers on the left" ✗

### Financial terminology
- **accounts payable (AP)** — spell out first; lead with a plain-language explanation before the abbreviation
- **accounts receivable (AR)** — same rule
- **ACH** — use "bank transfer" or "bank transfer (ACH)" in U.S. customer-facing copy; never use ACH outside the U.S.
- **account** — lowercase, not a proper noun (except "Intuit Account")

### Tone-adjacent word rules
- **simple/simply** — avoid. Subjective. If it's truly simple, it'll be self-evident.
- **sorry** — apologize only when we've genuinely failed. Not as filler.
- **since vs. because** — use "because" for cause/effect; "since" = time only
  - "Online payments are faster because the money goes directly into your account." ✓
  - "Online payments are faster since the money goes directly into your account." ✗

### Format
- **401(k)** — not 401K or 401k
- **AM/PM** — no periods, capitalize (not a.m./p.m.)
- **Social Security number / SSN** — spell out and capitalize; SSN only when space-constrained
