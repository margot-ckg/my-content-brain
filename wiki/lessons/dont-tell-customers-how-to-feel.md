# Don't tell customers how to feel

**Rule:** Never predict, prescribe, or assume a customer's emotional reaction. Respect their mood without imposing one.

**Why:** Customers will see through emotional projection. Telling someone "You're going to love this" or "Great news!" when the moment isn't universally positive erodes trust. It makes the product feel out of touch — or worse, manipulative.

**How to apply:**
- Describe what happened or what's available. Don't editorialize about whether it's good.
- "Great news! Your payroll system is modernizing." ✗ — change isn't always great news for everyone
- "We know change can be hard. We're here for you." ✓ — acknowledges reality
- "You're going to love our payroll solution!" ✗ — telling them how they'll feel
- "We simplify every part of getting paid, so you can focus on what matters." ✓ — states the benefit, lets them feel what they feel
- Avoid: "Don't worry" in serious or high-stakes situations. If you remove "don't worry" and the copy still works, you don't need it.
- In celebration copy: celebrate what they did, not how they should feel about it.
  - "You paid off your loan!" ✓
  - "You must be thrilled!" ✗
