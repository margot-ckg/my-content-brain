# No superlatives

**Rule:** Never use superlatives (world-class, best, top, fastest, ultimate, premier, most-trusted, amazing, great) in product copy unless backed by cited data.

**Why:** Superlatives ring alarm bells for customers, diminishing trust. They sound desperate. If you're world-class, you don't have to say it. Customers are smart — they see through the hype.

**How to apply:**
- If the opposite ("low quality," "slow," "mediocre") is something we'd never say, you're probably using hype. Cut it.
- "Good" often works better than "great." "Good" gets the job done. "Great" may oversell.
- Facts always beat superlatives. If QuickBooks is #1 in a category, cite the source and the date.
- If something is genuinely simple, it should be self-evident — you don't need to say "simple."
- Watch for: "You're going to love this" — also a superlative in disguise (predicting the customer's emotion).

**Examples:**
- "We'll take good care of your team" ✓
- "We'll take great care of your team" ✗
- "The right tools, right from the start" ✓
- "The best tools from the get-go" ✗
- "Pay your team with the #1 payroll service provider / Based on overall numbers of customers for QuickBooks payroll products as of 06/2022" ✓ (backed by data with citation)
