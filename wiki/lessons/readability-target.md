# Readability target: 5th–8th grade

**Rule:** Write at a 5th to 8th grade reading level (ages 10–14). Score with Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level or Gunning Fog Index before shipping.

**Why:** The average American reads at a 7th–8th grade level. Lower reading levels = more inclusive for neurodivergent readers, non-native English speakers, and anyone who is distracted, tired, or on mobile. Nielsen Norman Group found that people slow down on 12th-grade content on mobile — even people capable of reading it.

**How to apply:**
- Default to shorter sentences. Most copy under 12 words.
- Use shorter, everyday words: "use" not "utilize," "help" not "facilitate," "end" not "terminate."
- Studies show people skip words 8–9 letters long more than words 3–5 letters long.
- Use simple verb tenses: "you import" not "you'll be importing," "you got" not "you had been getting."
- Exception: Technical/professional audiences (accountants, developers) can handle domain jargon. Raise the level deliberately, not accidentally.
- Use readability tools as a guide, not a formula. Clear + familiar + short > a score.
