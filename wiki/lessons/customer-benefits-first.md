# Customer benefits before business goals

**Rule:** When writing product copy, always lead with what the customer gains — not what we offer, feature, or need them to do.

**Why:** Intuit's core value is Customer Obsession. Copy that prioritizes business metrics ("click here," "try this feature," "upgrade now") makes customers feel like a revenue target, not a person. Copy that leads with their benefit builds the relationship.

**How to apply:**
- Ask: "What does the customer get out of this?" — then lead with that.
- Ask: "Would we ever say the opposite of this?" — if not, you may be using filler praise.
- Feature → benefit reframe: "This feature tracks mileage automatically" → "You'll never miss a tax deduction."
- Upsell framing test: If it reads like we're squeezing revenue, rewrite it around their outcome.
  - "Only an extra $35/mo" ✗
  - "We'll be with you every step of the way" ✓
- In empty states and onboarding: describe what they'll be able to do, not what the product does.
- In error states: the benefit is recovery — focus on their next action and the reassurance that their work is safe.
